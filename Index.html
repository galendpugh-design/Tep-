Our story

Born from necessity.
Built on showing up.

A chance meeting in a Philadelphia subway station became one of the city's most active harm reduction organizations.

December 2020, Center City

It was cold. Six months into the COVID-19 pandemic, overdoses were higher than they'd been in years, and nearly every existing outreach organization had stopped going out.

Jen had been doing street outreach for years. Shannon had just started and ran out of supplies within an hour. Jen loaded her up from her car without hesitation. They exchanged numbers and kept showing up, together.

"We believe that people need people, and none of us can get through life alone."

Four months later, they filed articles of incorporation. 501(c)(3) by May 2021. First week at Love Park: 30 people served. Now: 300+ every Wednesday. 5 million meals. #2 safer-use supplier in Philadelphia. Zero missed outreach events, ever.

Mission

Expand access to harm reduction services and education across Philadelphia through naloxone, testing strips, safer use kits, and community presence.

Vision

A world without overdoses, where substance use is no longer stigmatized and every person receives the support and dignity they deserve.

Design rationale

Why this redesign
looks like this.

Every color and layout decision has a reason.

The palette, purple and teal, used deliberately

Warm, human, and restrained

01

Purple/teal gradient for momentum

The hero and inner page headers use a dynamic gradient that suggests movement and energy while staying sophisticated. It communicates that TEP is active, modern, and always moving forward.

02

Purple only on actions and accents

Purple appears on: all CTA buttons, typography highlights, badges, and team avatars. Every purple element reads as "this matters" or "act here" without needing to say so.

03

Cyan for warmth and humanity

The bright cyan accent appears in italicized headings and stat numbers, signaling the human, emotional core of the work. It's warm without being sentimental.

04

Lavender and Periwinkle gradients

Cards, mission boxes, and info sections use soft purple-to-cyan gradients, creating visual interest while keeping backgrounds restful. These sections feel inviting and safe.

05

White base stays foundational

Body sections still start from white. This ensures readability and focus, while color becomes a supporting player rather than the main event.

06

Lora and Inter, same partnership

Lora is literary and humanist. Inter is clean and neutral. Together they still communicate care and accessibility, now wrapped in a more modern color story.
